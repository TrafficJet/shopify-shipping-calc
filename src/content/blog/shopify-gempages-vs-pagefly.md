# GemPages vs PageFly: Best Shopify Page Builder 2026

Choosing between GemPages and PageFly comes down to your store size, budget, and how seriously you want to optimize conversions. This side-by-side comparison covers pricing, speed, integrations, A/B testing, and support so you can pick the right builder without wasting time on trial and error.

## GemPages vs PageFly: Quick Verdict

**GemPages** is best for growing stores that want built-in A/B testing, flat monthly pricing, and faster page load times. **PageFly** is best for beginners or small stores that only need a few polished landing pages and prefer a pay-as-you-grow pricing model.

Both apps rank among the highest-rated page builders on the Shopify App Store. GemPages holds a 4.9-star rating with over 4,500 reviews, while PageFly maintains a 4.9-star rating with over 12,000 reviews (Shopify App Store, as of 2026).

The trade-off is simple. GemPages gives you more conversion tools out of the box but costs more at scale. PageFly costs less if you only publish a handful of pages but lacks native A/B testing.

## Pricing: PageFly Starts Lower, but GemPages Can Save Money at Scale

GemPages uses flat monthly pricing across four tiers (as of 2026). The **Free** plan gives you one published page. The **Build** plan costs **$29/month** and unlocks unlimited published pages plus CRO-focused templates. The **Optimize** plan at **$59/month** adds A/B testing, page analytics, and priority support. **Enterprise** pricing is custom and includes a dedicated account manager.

PageFly works differently. The **Free** plan allows one published page. The **Pay-as-you-grow** model starts at **$24/month** for up to 10 published slots and scales to **$99/month** for unlimited pages and advanced features. Costs rise as you publish more pages. This catches merchants off guard when building out a full product catalog.

If you're launching your first store, PageFly's $24/month tier is cheaper than GemPages' $29/month Build plan. But if you're running dozens of landing pages and want A/B testing included, GemPages' $59/month Optimize plan is typically cheaper than pairing PageFly with a separate tool like VWO or Convert — those run $99–$199/month on their own.

Watch for hidden costs on both platforms. Some premium GemPages templates require a one-time purchase fee. PageFly charges no extra template fees, but collaborator seats for agencies may count against your plan limits. Neither app charges transaction fees on top of Shopify's standard rates.

Merchants who start with PageFly's lower tier often find that costs converge with GemPages once they exceed 10 published pages and need external testing tools. Run the math based on your current page count before committing.

## Templates and Design Flexibility: More Options vs. a Cleaner Editor

GemPages offers over 100 templates covering fashion, beauty, electronics, food and beverage, and fitness niches. Templates are grouped by page type — homepage, product, landing, collection, and blog — so you can filter quickly. The designs lean modern and conversion-focused, with built-in sections for testimonials, image galleries, and countdown timers.

PageFly's library is slightly larger at 130+ templates and includes strong mobile-optimized designs. Many PageFly templates are built with Shopify 2.0 section architecture. Because of this, they blend with your existing theme sections and blocks without extra configuration. Mobile layouts are a real strength here. PageFly gives you a separate mobile editor with granular control over element visibility and spacing.

Both editors use drag-and-drop interfaces that non-coders can handle. GemPages feels snappier and offers a cleaner UI with fewer nested menus. PageFly has more individual element options, which adds flexibility but also steepens the learning curve. New users sometimes spend 30+ minutes locating specific settings buried in nested panels. Both apps let you add custom CSS and Liquid code blocks, so developers have full control when the visual editor isn't enough.

Skincare brand *Herbivore Botanicals* used GemPages to build product education landing pages with embedded video sections and quiz widgets, reporting a 22% lift in add-to-cart rate compared to their standard theme pages (GemPages Case Studies, 2025). That kind of modular page building — mixing educational content with conversion elements — is where GemPages' drag-and-drop widget library does well.

Both builders are fully compatible with Shopify 2.0 themes. You can use app blocks, metafields, and dynamic sources within either tool without breaking your theme's native functionality.

## Page Speed and Core Web Vitals: GemPages Produces Lighter Pages

Page speed directly affects your Shopify store's search rankings and conversion rate. Google's research shows that a one-second delay in mobile load time can reduce conversions by up to 20% (Google/Deloitte, "Milliseconds Make Millions," 2020). Both GemPages and PageFly inject additional scripts into your pages, but the amount of code bloat differs meaningfully.

In independent testing on a clean Dawn theme with a standard product page, GemPages pages scored between **75 and 90** on Google PageSpeed Insights (mobile), while PageFly pages scored between **65 and 82** (PageSpeed Insights benchmarks, 2026). GemPages achieves lighter output because it renders fewer DOM elements and defers non-critical scripts more aggressively.

PageFly tends to produce element-heavy pages, especially when you use multiple animated sections, accordion blocks, or embedded third-party widgets. Each additional element adds to the DOM size. This can push your Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) — the time it takes for the largest visible element to render — and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) — unexpected layout movement during loading — into the "needs improvement" range on Core Web Vitals reports.

Both apps support image lazy loading and WebP format delivery. GemPages also auto-compresses images during upload.

Before committing to either builder, install both free plans and build the same test page. Run each through Google PageSpeed Insights on mobile and desktop to see how your specific theme and content combination performs. Merchants who skip this step often discover speed problems only after building out 20+ pages — at which point switching builders means rebuilding everything.

## Shopify Integrations: Native Widgets vs. Manual Embeds

GemPages integrates natively with Klaviyo, Judge.me, Loox, and other popular Shopify apps through built-in widgets. You can drop a Loox review carousel or a Klaviyo email signup form directly into your page layout without pasting embed codes. GemPages also supports native product upsell and cross-sell blocks tied to Shopify's product data.

PageFly takes a third-party embed approach for most integrations. You can add Klaviyo forms, Judge.me widgets, or Yotpo reviews using HTML or Liquid embed blocks. This works, but requires more manual setup than GemPages' drag-and-drop widgets. PageFly does have direct integrations with a handful of apps, though the list is shorter.

For Shopify Markets and multi-language stores, both builders support multi-currency and language translation apps like Langify and Weglot. GemPages handles translated content through Shopify's native translation API. PageFly requires you to duplicate pages for each market in some configurations — a limitation that adds maintenance overhead for stores selling in three or more regions.

Both tools let you build product pages, landing pages, collection pages, blog posts, and custom pages. GemPages also includes a dedicated "funnel" page type for post-purchase upsells when paired with apps like ReConvert.

DTC coffee brand *Trade Coffee* uses PageFly for subscription landing pages integrated with Recharge, pairing embedded Klaviyo forms to capture email leads before the product pitch (PageFly Showcase, 2025). This embed-based workflow suits stores comfortable with basic HTML setup.

## A/B Testing and Conversion Optimization: GemPages Has a Clear Edge

GemPages includes built-in A/B testing starting on the **Optimize plan ($59/month)**. You can split-test different page versions, headlines, hero images, or CTA button placements directly inside the editor. GemPages tracks impressions, clicks, and conversion events, then declares a statistical winner once your sample size is large enough. This eliminates the need for a separate testing tool.

PageFly does not offer native A/B testing. To run split tests on PageFly pages, you need a third-party tool like VWO, Convert, or Optimizely — that adds $99–$199/month to your tech stack (VWO and Convert pricing pages, as of 2026). This makes PageFly less practical for stores that want to iterate on page performance without stacking subscriptions.

Both builders include conversion-focused elements like countdown timers, sticky add-to-cart bars, and upsell blocks. GemPages' analytics dashboard shows per-page metrics including bounce rate, scroll depth, and revenue attribution. PageFly's analytics are more limited — you'll rely on Google Analytics 4 or Shopify's built-in reports for most conversion data.

A Shopify Plus apparel brand used GemPages A/B testing to compare a long-form product page against a short-form version. The short-form variant increased conversions by 17% over 30 days across 14,000 sessions (GemPages, 2025). That kind of rapid iteration is hard to replicate in PageFly without external tools and extra monthly spend.

One caveat: GemPages' A/B testing is functional but less sophisticated than dedicated platforms like VWO or Optimizely. It handles page-level split tests well. But if you need multivariate testing, audience segmentation, or advanced statistical models, a dedicated CRO tool may still be necessary regardless of which builder you choose.

## Customer Support: PageFly Wins on Accessibility, GemPages on Enterprise Service

PageFly's biggest advantage is its **24/7 live chat support**, available on all plans including the free tier. Average response times hover around 2–3 minutes during peak hours (PageFly, 2026). Support agents can also help with basic page design and troubleshooting, which is valuable if you're not a designer.

GemPages provides live chat on the Optimize and Enterprise plans. Build plan users get email support with typical response times of 6–12 hours. Free plan users are limited to help center articles and community resources. This support gap on lower tiers matters if you expect hands-on help during setup.

Both apps maintain extensive help centers with video tutorials, step-by-step guides, and template walkthroughs. PageFly's YouTube channel has more content overall, while GemPages' documentation is better organized by use case. PageFly also runs an active Facebook community group with over 10,000 members where merchants share page designs and troubleshooting tips.

For enterprise-level support, GemPages assigns a dedicated customer success manager and offers priority Slack or Zoom onboarding. PageFly's top-tier plan includes priority support but does not include a dedicated account representative. Merchants who have worked with both platforms at the enterprise level often report that GemPages' dedicated CSM shortens onboarding from weeks to days.

## GemPages vs PageFly: Who Should Use Each?

**Choose GemPages if** you run a growing DTC brand, want built-in A/B testing, prefer predictable flat pricing, and care about page speed. GemPages is the stronger choice for stores doing $10K+/month that need to test and optimize landing pages regularly. Shopify Plus merchants benefit from the Enterprise plan's dedicated support and advanced CRO tools.

**Choose PageFly if** you're a beginner with a small store, need one or two polished landing pages, and want 24/7 live chat support on every plan. PageFly's lower starting price and extensive template library make it a practical starting point for new stores or side projects. It's also a solid pick for dropshipping stores where you're testing products quickly and don't need A/B testing infrastructure.

If you're migrating from one builder to the other, pages don't transfer between apps. You'll need to rebuild your layouts from scratch. Both tools let you unpublish and archive pages, so you can keep your old builder installed while you transition without losing existing pages.

**Final recommendation:** Match your builder to your revenue stage. Under $5K/month? Start with PageFly's free or $24 plan. Over $10K/month and actively optimizing? GemPages' Optimize plan at $59/month typically pays for itself with a single winning A/B test. Between those stages, the free plans on both apps give you enough room to evaluate before spending.

## How to Get Started with Your Chosen Builder

Install either app directly from the Shopify App Store (**Shopify Admin → Apps → Search**). After installation, connect it to your active theme — both apps auto-detect Shopify 2.0 themes and configure themselves accordingly. Select a template or start with a blank page, customize it in the drag-and-drop editor, and hit publish.

Start with the free plan on whichever builder you choose. Build one page, test it for speed and conversions over two weeks, then decide whether to upgrade. GemPages offers a 10-day free trial on paid plans. PageFly lets you preview unlimited draft pages for free before publishing.

Pick the builder that fits where your store is right now, not where you hope it'll be in a year. You can switch later — just budget time to rebuild your pages if you do.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is GemPages or PageFly better for Shopify in 2026?
GemPages is better for stores that want built-in A/B testing and flat monthly pricing. PageFly suits beginners who only need a few landing pages and prefer a pay-per-page model. Both hold 4.9-star ratings on the Shopify App Store as of 2026.

### Does GemPages slow down your Shopify store?
GemPages is generally lighter than PageFly, scoring 75–90 on PageSpeed Insights mobile tests compared to PageFly's 65–82 range (2026 benchmarks on Dawn theme). Results depend on how many elements and apps you load onto each page.

### Can I use both GemPages and PageFly on the same Shopify store?
Technically yes, but running two page builder apps adds code to your theme and can degrade page speed. Most merchants pick one and stick with it.

### Does PageFly have a free plan?
Yes. PageFly's free plan lets you publish one page at no cost. Additional published pages require a paid plan starting at $24/month (as of 2026).

### Which page builder works best with Shopify Plus?
Both support Shopify Plus. GemPages offers enterprise plans with a dedicated customer success manager and priority onboarding. PageFly also has Plus-compatible features, but GemPages' built-in A/B testing gives Plus merchants more optimization tools without third-party subscriptions.

### Do GemPages or PageFly support Shopify 2.0 themes?
Both are fully compatible with Shopify 2.0 themes as of 2026, allowing you to use sections, blocks, metafields, and dynamic sources alongside your page builder content.
