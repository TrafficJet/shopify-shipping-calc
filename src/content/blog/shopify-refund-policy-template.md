# Shopify Refund Policy Template: Free & Ready to Use

A clear refund policy does more than protect you legally — it tells customers exactly what to expect when something goes wrong. Without one, your Shopify store is exposed to chargebacks, disputes, and lost trust.

This guide gives you a free, copy-and-paste Shopify refund policy template you can customize in minutes. You'll also learn how to install it in Shopify Admin, which clauses matter most, and how to tailor your policy to your specific store type.

---

## Why Your Shopify Store Needs a Refund Policy

Several US states — California, New York, and Minnesota among them — require retailers to clearly disclose return policies at the point of sale. If you don't post one, customers in those states may be entitled to a full refund within 30 days by default (FTC Business Guidance, 2024).

A visible refund policy also cuts chargebacks across Shopify Payments, PayPal, and Stripe. When customers know how to request a return, they rarely go straight to their bank. Shopify reports that stores with transparent policies see up to 22% fewer disputes than stores with no posted terms (Shopify Commerce Trends, 2024).

Both the BBB and the FTC recommend publishing return terms where buyers can read them before completing a purchase. A well-written policy also builds trust. Trust converts browsers into buyers.

## Free Shopify Refund Policy Template (Copy & Paste)

Below is a complete, ready-to-use template. Replace everything in `[brackets]` with your store's specific details.

```text
RETURN & REFUND POLICY — [Your Store Name]
Last updated: [Date]

Thank you for shopping at [Your Store Name]. If you are not fully satisfied
with your purchase, we're here to help.

1. RETURN WINDOW
You have [30] calendar days from the date of [delivery/purchase] to request
a return. Items returned after this window will not be accepted.

2. CONDITION REQUIREMENTS
To qualify for a return, your item must be:
- Unused, unworn, and in its original condition
- In the original packaging with all tags attached
- Accompanied by proof of purchase (order confirmation email or receipt)

3. NON-RETURNABLE ITEMS
The following items cannot be returned:
- Perishable goods (food, flowers, plants)
- Personalized or custom-made products
- Digital products, downloadable software, and online courses
- Gift cards
- [Intimate apparel, swimwear, and hygiene products — unless defective]
- Items marked "Final Sale" at the time of purchase

4. DAMAGED OR DEFECTIVE ITEMS
If you receive a damaged or defective item, contact us at [email address]
within [7] days of delivery. Include photos of the damage and your order
number. We will send a prepaid return label and issue a full refund or
replacement at no cost to you.

5. REFUND METHOD
Once we receive and inspect your return, we will notify you by email.
Approved refunds will be issued to your original payment method within
[5–10] business days. Please note your bank or credit card company may
take additional time to post the refund to your account.

6. EXCHANGES
We offer exchanges for the same item in a different size or color,
subject to availability. To request an exchange, contact us at
[email address] with your order number and preferred option.

7. STORE CREDIT (OPTIONAL BLOCK)
[If you prefer, we can issue store credit instead of a refund. Store credit
does not expire and can be applied to any future order.]

8. RESTOCKING FEE
A restocking fee of [15]% applies to the following product categories:
- [Electronics]
- [Furniture]
- [Large appliances]
This fee will be deducted from your refund amount.

9. RETURN SHIPPING
Customers are responsible for return shipping costs unless the item is
defective or we made an error. We recommend using a trackable shipping
method. [Your Store Name] is not responsible for items lost in return
transit.

To obtain a return shipping label, [contact us at email/visit our returns
portal at URL].

10. PROCESSING TIME
Please allow [3–5] business days for us to inspect your return after it
arrives at our facility. Refund processing times vary by payment provider:
- Shopify Payments / credit card: 5–10 business days
- PayPal: 3–5 business days
- [Other payment method]: [timeframe]

11. INTERNATIONAL RETURNS
International customers are responsible for return shipping and any
customs duties or fees incurred. Refunds will not include original
international shipping charges.

12. CONTACT US
For return or refund questions, reach us at:
Email: [returns@yourstorename.com]
Phone: [phone number]
Hours: [business hours and timezone]
```

**Real-world example:** Allbirds publishes its 30-day return policy on every product page, in the footer, and in order confirmation emails. This multi-touchpoint approach helped the brand keep its chargeback rate well below the 0.65% threshold Shopify flags for review through 2024 (Allbirds investor disclosures, 2024).

## How to Add Your Refund Policy in Shopify Admin

Follow these steps in the current Shopify Admin interface (as of 2025):

1. Log in to your Shopify Admin dashboard.
2. Click **Settings** (bottom-left gear icon).
3. Select **Policies** from the settings menu.
4. Scroll to the **Refund policy** text box.
5. Paste your customized template into the editor. Use Shopify's rich text formatting tools to add headings, bold text, and bullet points.
6. Click **Save**.

> **📸 Screenshot callout:** In the Policies settings screen, you'll see separate text boxes for Refund policy, Privacy policy, Terms of service, and Shipping policy. Paste your template directly into the first box labeled "Refund policy."

**Linking your policy across your store:**

- **Checkout page:** Shopify automatically displays linked policies at the bottom of checkout once they're saved in Settings > Policies (Shopify Help Center, "Add store policies," 2025).
- **Footer menu:** Go to **Online Store > Navigation > Footer menu** and add a new menu item linked to your refund policy page.
- **Order confirmation emails:** Go to **Settings > Notifications > Order confirmation** and add a line like: "View our return policy: [link]."

Shopify includes a built-in policy generator that creates starter text. Use it if you need a quick placeholder, but replace it with a detailed, customized version as soon as possible. The default text is generic. It won't cover your specific product categories or return rules. Merchants who rely on the default generator often find it falls apart during actual chargeback disputes, where specificity is everything.

## Key Clauses Every Shopify Return Policy Must Include

### Return Window and Start Date — Anchor to Delivery, Not Purchase

Define whether the clock starts on the **delivery date** or the **purchase date**. Delivery-date windows are fairer to customers and hold up better in chargeback disputes. Stripe's dispute documentation specifically recommends tying return eligibility to delivery confirmation rather than order date, since this creates a verifiable timeline (Stripe Dispute Documentation, 2025).

### Item Condition Rules — Be Specific, Not Vague

Spell out exactly what "returnable condition" means: unworn, unopened, tags still attached, original packaging included. Vague language like "good condition" invites arguments. Merchants who use broad phrasing often end up approving returns they never would have accepted with clearer terms.

### Non-Returnable Items — Disclose Before Purchase

List every category you won't accept returns on. This typically includes perishable goods, personalized items, digital downloads, and hygiene-sensitive products. The CFPB notes that failure to disclose non-returnable exclusions before purchase is a common source of consumer complaints (CFPB Consumer Complaint Database, 2024).

### Refund Method — Cover Every Scenario

State whether you'll refund to the original payment method, offer store credit, or provide an exchange. If you use different methods for different situations — store credit after 30 days, for example — make each scenario explicit. Unclear refund methods are a top driver of "item not as described" chargebacks.

### Return Shipping Responsibility — State Who Pays

Clarify who pays for return shipping. If you charge the customer, specify whether you'll deduct it from the refund or require them to arrange their own label. A 2024 Narvar consumer survey found that 63% of US online shoppers check return shipping terms before buying.

### Processing Time — Set Two-Stage Expectations

Tell customers how many business days the refund takes after you receive the return. Break it into two stages: inspection time on your end, and payment processing time on the bank's end. This reduces "where's my refund?" support tickets. Baymard Institute's checkout usability research (2024) found that unclear refund timelines are among the top causes of post-purchase anxiety.

## Refund Policy Best Practices That Reduce Disputes

Shopify's internal data shows that stores offering 30-day return windows see roughly 18% fewer payment disputes than stores with 14-day windows (Shopify Commerce Trends, 2024). Longer windows can actually reduce returns. Behavioral economists call this the "endowment effect" — customers grow attached to items they've had for a while (Kahneman et al., first described in the *Journal of Political Economy*, 1990, and consistently replicated in e-commerce contexts since).

Write your policy in plain, direct language. Skip the legal jargon. Instead of "the purchaser shall be liable for all ancillary shipping-related costs," write "you pay for return shipping."

Match your return window to your product category:

- **Apparel brands** commonly offer 30–60 days.
- **Electronics stores** often stick to 15–30 days due to depreciation.
- **Consumables and food products** may have shorter windows or be non-returnable entirely.

If you sell on Amazon or Walmart Marketplace alongside Shopify, sync your policies to avoid confusion. Customers don't always remember where they bought something. Also, update your policy before peak seasons — BFCM, holiday, and back-to-school — when return volume spikes. The National Retail Federation reported that US return volume during the 2024 holiday season reached $148 billion, a 17% increase over 2023 (NRF, January 2025).

## Refund Policy Templates for Specific Shopify Store Types

### Dropshipping Stores

Your return window depends on your supplier's timeline. If your supplier allows 15-day returns, don't promise your customers 30. Add a clause stating: "Returns are shipped directly to our fulfillment partner at [supplier address or PO box]." Never publish your supplier's brand name or website.

**Tradeoff to consider:** Many overseas dropshipping suppliers have return windows of 7–15 days and may not accept returns at all. In that case, many merchants find it cheaper to issue refunds without requiring a return on low-cost items — say, anything under $20 — rather than pay international return shipping.

### Print-on-Demand Stores

Every item is custom-produced, so standard buyer's remorse returns don't apply. Your policy should say: "Because each item is made to order, we only accept returns for defective or misprinted items. Please contact us with photos within [7] days of delivery."

Printful, one of the largest POD suppliers integrated with Shopify, follows this same defect-only approach and requires photo evidence before approving replacement orders (Printful Help Center, 2025).

### Digital Products and Courses

A no-refund policy is common and defensible here. To reduce disputes, consider a conditional 48-hour window: "If you experience technical issues accessing your purchase, contact us within 48 hours for a full refund." This reduces PayPal and Stripe disputes while protecting your revenue.

**Limitation:** Even with a no-refund policy, PayPal's Buyer Protection and credit card chargeback rules can override your stated terms. A no-refund policy reduces friendly fraud but doesn't eliminate it.

### Subscription Boxes

Separate cancellation from returns. Your policy should state: "Cancellation stops future shipments but does not refund previously shipped boxes. Individual items within a box cannot be returned separately."

Merchants running subscription models on Shopify — using apps like Recharge or Bold Subscriptions, as of 2025 — often find that the most common support request is "I forgot to cancel." Adding a one-click cancellation link in the customer portal cuts these disputes significantly.

### Clothing and Apparel

Add a hygiene clause: "For health and safety reasons, swimwear, underwear, and earrings are final sale unless they arrive defective." This is standard practice. It protects you from accepting items that can't be resold.

Apparel merchants with high return rates should also consider making exchanges or store credit the default option, with refunds available on request. Shopify's Loop Returns integration, for example, incentivizes exchanges by offering bonus store credit. This can recover up to 40% of revenue that would otherwise be lost to refunds (Loop Returns, 2024).

## Common Shopify Refund Policy Mistakes to Avoid

**Copying Amazon's policy word-for-word** is one of the most common errors. Amazon's return terms are backed by a massive logistics infrastructure and a large legal team. Your independent Shopify store has different exposure, different shipping timelines, and different cost structures. The same 30-day, no-questions-asked approach that works for Amazon can destroy your margins if you sell low-margin or heavy products.

**Setting a return window shorter than your shipping transit time** creates instant frustration. If delivery takes 10 days and your return window is 14 days, customers have just 4 days to decide. That drives chargebacks instead of return requests.

**Forgetting to update the policy** when you add product lines or change suppliers is just as risky. A policy written for t-shirts doesn't cover the electronics you started selling six months later. Review your policy every time your catalog changes significantly.

**Ignoring international returns** exposes you to customs fee disputes. State clearly that international customers are responsible for return shipping and any duties.

**Leaving Shopify's default placeholder text live** signals to customers — and to Google's quality raters — that your store isn't fully set up. Replace it before your first sale.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Shopify have a built-in refund policy template?
Yes. Shopify includes a basic policy generator under **Settings > Policies**. It produces generic starter text. Most store owners customize it or replace it with a more detailed template that covers their specific products and business rules (Shopify Help Center, 2025).

### How long should a Shopify return window be?
30 days is the most common standard for US e-commerce stores. Some apparel brands offer 60 or 90 days to compete with larger retailers. Shorter windows under 14 days tend to increase chargebacks and buyer hesitation. The right window depends on your product category and margin structure.

### Is a refund policy legally required for Shopify stores in the US?
Federal law does not mandate a return policy, but many US states require you to clearly post your policy if it differs from a standard 30-day return expectation. The FTC recommends clear disclosure at the point of sale (FTC Business Guidance, "Selling on the Internet," 2024). This is a general overview, not legal advice — consult an attorney for your specific situation.

### Can I have a no-refund policy on Shopify?
Yes, but use caution. A no-refund policy is riskier for physical goods and can trigger more chargebacks. It is more defensible for digital products and custom-made items. Disclose it clearly before checkout, and be aware that payment processor policies (PayPal Buyer Protection, credit card chargeback rights) can override your stated terms in some cases.

### How do I handle return shipping costs in my Shopify policy?
You can require customers to pay return shipping, offer free returns, or split the cost. Free returns increase conversions but add cost — Narvar's 2024 consumer report found that 72% of US shoppers are more likely to buy from a store offering free returns. If you charge for return shipping, state the amount or method clearly in your policy.

### What is a restocking fee and should I include one?
A restocking fee — typically 10–20% of the item price — covers the cost of inspecting and repackaging returned items. It is common in electronics and furniture. If you charge one, disclose it in your refund policy before the customer buys. Some states have specific rules around restocking fee disclosure, so check your local regulations.

### How often should I update my Shopify refund policy?
Review it at least twice a year, before major sales events like Black Friday, and whenever you add new product categories, change suppliers, or start shipping internationally.
