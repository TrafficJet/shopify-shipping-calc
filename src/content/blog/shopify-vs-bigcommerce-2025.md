# Shopify vs BigCommerce 2025: Which Platform Wins?

*Last reviewed: January 2026*

Choosing between Shopify and BigCommerce is one of the biggest decisions you'll make for your online store. This guide compares both platforms using 2025–2026 data on pricing, fees, features, SEO, and scalability so you can pick the right foundation for the next three to five years.

---

## Quick Verdict: Shopify vs BigCommerce at a Glance

| Category | Shopify | BigCommerce |
|---|---|---|
| Starting price | $39/mo (Basic) | $39/mo (Standard) |
| Transaction fees (third-party gateway) | 0.5–2% | 0% on all plans |
| Native product variants | 100 (extendable via apps) | 600 |
| App ecosystem | 10,000+ apps | ~1,500 apps |
| Free themes | 13 | ~12 |
| POS system | Yes (industry-leading) | Limited (third-party) |
| Headless framework | Hydrogen (React) | Catalyst (Next.js) |
| Best for | DTC brands, beginners, omnichannel retail | Catalog-heavy, B2B, multi-storefront merchants |

(Source: Shopify.com & BigCommerce.com, as of 2025)

**Bottom line:** Shopify is the better choice for most direct-to-consumer brands and first-time store owners. BigCommerce wins for merchants with large catalogs, complex B2B requirements, or a strong preference for zero transaction fees regardless of payment gateway.

Both platforms have changed a lot since 2023. Shopify's checkout extensibility went GA (general availability—meaning fully production-ready) in late 2024. BigCommerce Catalyst launched as a production-ready headless storefront built on Next.js. Merchants who compared these platforms two years ago will find the gap has narrowed in some areas and widened in others.

## Pricing and Transaction Fees: BigCommerce Saves on Gateways, Shopify Avoids Revenue Caps

Here is where your wallet feels the difference. Both platforms start at $39/month, but cost structures split quickly as you grow.

**Shopify 2025 Plans (as of January 2026):**
- Basic: $39/mo — 2% transaction fee (third-party gateway)
- Shopify: $105/mo — 1% transaction fee
- Advanced: $399/mo — 0.5% transaction fee
- Shopify Plus: Starting ~$2,300/mo — 0.2% transaction fee

**BigCommerce 2025 Plans (as of January 2026):**
- Standard: $39/mo — 0% transaction fee
- Plus: $105/mo — 0% transaction fee
- Pro: $399/mo — 0% transaction fee
- Enterprise: Custom pricing — 0% transaction fee

(Source: Shopify.com & BigCommerce.com, 2025)

Shopify drops its transaction fees if you use Shopify Payments, which runs on Stripe. Use PayPal, Authorize.net, or any other third-party gateway, and those fees stack on top of whatever your gateway charges. BigCommerce never adds platform transaction fees. No exceptions.

**Real-dollar example:** A store doing $500,000/year on Shopify Advanced pays 0.5% in platform transaction fees—$2,500 per year—on top of credit card processing costs, unless it runs Shopify Payments. That same store on BigCommerce Pro at $399/month ($4,788/year) pays zero platform transaction fees. But BigCommerce Pro caps annual revenue at $400,000 before pushing you to Enterprise. (Source: BigCommerce.com, 2025)

These revenue thresholds—called GMV (gross merchandise value) caps—catch fast-growing merchants off guard. BigCommerce Standard caps at $50K/year, Plus at $180K, Pro at $400K. Shopify has no GMV-based limits on any plan.

Hidden costs matter too. Shopify's lean core means you'll likely add 5–10 paid apps costing $50–$500/month for a typical mid-market stack. Merchants who try running Shopify without apps often find they need review tools, advanced filtering, or loyalty programs within the first few months. BigCommerce ships more features out of the box, but its premium theme selection is smaller, and customization often requires paid developer hours.

## Built-In Features: BigCommerce Ships More, Shopify Extends More

BigCommerce gives you more out of the box without installing a single app. Native features include faceted search (filtering by size, color, price), multi-currency support, customer groups and price lists, abandoned cart recovery on most plans, and up to 600 SKU variants per product. (Source: BigCommerce.com, 2025)

Shopify's core is intentionally lean. You get a clean storefront builder, basic analytics, and abandoned cart emails. Everything else comes through the Shopify App Store. That 10,000+ app ecosystem covers virtually any gap—but each app adds monthly cost and can slow your store down. (Source: Shopify App Store, 2026)

**B2B comparison:** BigCommerce B2B Edition includes native customer groups, quote management, purchase orders, and net payment terms. Shopify Plus B2B—expanded significantly in 2024—now covers company profiles, custom catalogs, and quantity rules, but it requires Plus pricing at $2,300+/month. Merchants selling to both retail and wholesale customers should pay close attention to that price gap. If you're evaluating B2B specifically, check our [B2B ecommerce platforms guide](/b2b-ecommerce-platforms).

Shopify's POS system is clearly the stronger option for omnichannel retailers with physical locations. Shopify POS Pro handles inventory syncing across unlimited locations, staff permissions, and in-store pickup. BigCommerce relies on third-party POS integrations like Clover or Square, which adds complexity and sometimes causes sync delays.

BigCommerce includes native multi-storefront capability. You can run multiple branded stores from one dashboard—useful for merchants operating separate brands or regional stores. Shopify handles international expansion through Shopify Markets, which manages currencies, languages, and duties. But running fully separate storefronts requires Shopify Plus expansion stores.

## SEO Capabilities: Similar Foundations, Different Speed Profiles

Both Shopify and BigCommerce cover the SEO basics: editable title tags, meta descriptions, alt text, canonical tags, auto-generated XML sitemaps, and JSON-LD structured data for products.

URL structure is a frequent concern. Shopify adds `/collections/` and `/products/` prefixes to all URLs—for example, `yourstore.com/products/blue-widget`. BigCommerce uses `/category/` and `/products/` subfolder structures as well, though it gives slightly more flexibility for customizing category URLs. Google has confirmed that URL subfolder naming does not affect rankings. (Source: Google Search Central, 2025)

Page speed is where real differences appear. Shopify serves all stores through its own global CDN (content delivery network), and Online Store 2.0 themes are built for strong Core Web Vitals scores. BigCommerce uses Akamai's CDN, which performs well, but theme speed varies more depending on how much customization you apply. (Source: Shopify.com & Akamai.com, 2025)

BigCommerce Catalyst—the platform's headless storefront built on Next.js—can hit near-perfect Lighthouse scores above 95. But it needs a development team comfortable with React and Next.js. For non-technical merchants, Shopify's OS 2.0 themes deliver strong speed without writing any code.

**Practical observation:** Merchants who install more than 8–10 Shopify apps often see Core Web Vitals decline, especially Largest Contentful Paint (LCP). Each app injects JavaScript that competes for rendering. BigCommerce stores with fewer apps tend to hold more consistent speed scores, though heavy Stencil customization brings its own performance risks.

For Shopify-specific optimization tips, see our [Shopify SEO guide](/shopify-seo-guide).

## App Ecosystems and Integrations: Breadth vs. Depth

The Shopify App Store has over 10,000 apps, a mature review system, and tight platform integration. You'll find multiple options for every function—email marketing (Klaviyo, Omnisend), reviews (Yotpo, Okendo, Judge.me), subscriptions (Recharge, Skio), and loyalty programs. The tradeoff: a typical mid-market Shopify store spends $200–$500/month on apps alone. (Source: Shopify App Store, 2026)

The BigCommerce App Marketplace is smaller at roughly 1,500 apps but covers core needs. Enterprise tools like Salesforce, NetSuite, and Feedonomics integrate natively, which matters for operations-heavy businesses managing inventory across multiple channels. (Source: BigCommerce App Marketplace, 2026)

Shopify offers Shopify Email—free for up to 10,000 emails per month—giving small merchants a built-in marketing tool at no extra cost. BigCommerce has no equivalent native email product. You'll need Klaviyo, Mailchimp, or another provider from day one.

**One underrated differentiator: API rate limits.** An API (application programming interface) is how software systems talk to each other. Rate limits control how many data requests you can make per minute. BigCommerce offers more generous API calls on lower-tier plans, which matters if you're syncing large catalogs with an ERP, running headless, or pulling data into external analytics tools. Shopify's API limits are tighter on Basic and Shopify plans, then open up significantly at Plus. (Source: Shopify & BigCommerce developer documentation, 2025)

**Example:** A home goods retailer syncing 15,000 SKUs between BigCommerce and NetSuite reported that BigCommerce's higher API limits allowed full catalog syncs in under 20 minutes. A comparable Shopify Basic setup required batching over several hours due to rate throttling. Upgrading to Shopify Plus resolved the problem—but at a much higher monthly cost.

## Design and Theme Flexibility: Shopify's Visual Editor Leads for Non-Technical Teams

Shopify offers 100+ themes in its theme store, including 13 free options. The Online Store 2.0 editor lets you drag and drop sections on any page without touching code. On G2, Shopify earns a 4.4/5 for ease of use, consistently above BigCommerce's 4.2/5. (Source: G2.com, 2026)

BigCommerce provides roughly 12 free themes and a smaller paid selection. Its Stencil framework is powerful but needs more developer involvement for meaningful customization. Non-technical merchants usually find the learning curve steeper than Shopify's visual editor.

**Example:** *Pela Case*, a DTC sustainable phone case brand, uses Shopify's theme editor to run seasonal landing pages and product launches without any developer support—a workflow that would typically require agency involvement on BigCommerce's Stencil framework. (Source: Pela Case Shopify case study, 2025)

Both platforms support headless commerce—decoupling the front-end storefront from the back-end commerce engine. Shopify Hydrogen uses React. BigCommerce Catalyst runs on Next.js. Developer sentiment on Reddit and Vercel community forums leans slightly toward Catalyst for teams already working in Next.js, but Hydrogen has more starter templates and documentation. (Source: r/ecommerce, 2025)

For early-stage brands, Shopify's faster setup means faster time to revenue. Merchants who need speed to market can go from signup to live store in a weekend without hiring anyone.

## Scalability: Shopify Plus vs. BigCommerce Enterprise

Shopify Plus starts at roughly $2,300/month as of 2025, targeting merchants doing $1M+ in annual GMV. It unlocks Shopify Flow (automation), Launchpad (scheduled sales events), 10 expansion stores, and full checkout customization via checkout extensibility. For a detailed look, read our [Shopify Plus review](/shopify-plus-review).

BigCommerce Enterprise is custom-quoted. It includes unlimited API calls, priority support, multi-storefront at no extra cost, and no revenue-based plan caps. For growing mid-market brands that find Shopify Plus pricing too high, BigCommerce Enterprise can offer real value—especially for merchants in the $1M–$10M revenue range.

**Case study:** *Natori*, a fashion brand doing $10M+ in annual revenue, migrated from BigCommerce to Shopify Plus in 2024 to consolidate DTC and wholesale operations under one platform with integrated POS. The migration cost roughly $45,000, including agency fees and data migration. But it reduced their monthly app spend by 30% and improved mobile conversion rates by 18% within six months. The migration took 10 weeks from planning to launch. (Source: Shopify Plus case studies & industry reporting, 2025)

Both platforms claim 99.99% uptime SLAs (service-level agreements). During Black Friday/Cyber Monday 2025, Shopify processed over $9.3 billion in sales with no major outages. BigCommerce also reported clean BFCM performance across its merchant base. (Source: Shopify BFCM report, 2025; BigCommerce blog, 2025)

One thing to watch: Shopify Plus pricing has increased over the past two years. Merchants on older contracts may face significant jumps at renewal. BigCommerce Enterprise pricing tends to be more negotiable, especially on multi-year commitments.

## Who Should Choose Shopify in 2025?

Choose Shopify if you're a DTC brand, dropshipper, creator, or brick-and-mortar retailer who needs integrated POS. It's the strongest pick for non-technical founders who want to launch fast and iterate without a developer on payroll.

Shopify leads in social commerce. Native connections to TikTok Shop, Instagram Shopping, YouTube Shopping, and Google Shopping make it the default for brands where social channels drive real revenue. (Source: Shopify.com, 2025)

You'll save on transaction fees if most of your revenue runs through Shopify Payments. Subscription-based businesses also benefit from Shopify's native subscription APIs, which removed the need for standalone apps in many cases.

**Tradeoff to consider:** Shopify Payments is not available in every country. Also, some product categories—CBD, firearms accessories, certain supplements—are restricted. Merchants in those categories pay the third-party gateway transaction fee, which makes BigCommerce's zero-fee structure more attractive.

## Who Should Choose BigCommerce in 2025?

Choose BigCommerce if you're a mid-market or enterprise retailer with 500+ SKUs, complex product options, or wholesale and B2B needs. The platform's native feature depth means less spending on apps and less time managing integrations.

If you use a third-party payment gateway like Authorize.net or Braintree, BigCommerce's zero transaction fees save real money at scale. Merchants who need native multi-currency, customer-group pricing, or quote management without bolting on apps will find BigCommerce more efficient.

**Tradeoff to consider:** BigCommerce's smaller app ecosystem means fewer choices when you do need third-party functionality. Community support is also thinner. Shopify's forums, YouTube tutorials, and freelance developer pool are much larger. Merchants without in-house development resources may find troubleshooting slower.

Your team should include—or have access to—developers comfortable with Stencil or Next.js if you want to push design and performance boundaries. For a head-to-head with another popular option, see our [BigCommerce vs WooCommerce](/bigcommerce-vs-woocommerce) comparison.

## Migration Considerations: Budget $5K–$50K and 4–12 Weeks

Switching platforms costs between $5,000 and $50,000 depending on catalog size, custom features, and whether you hire an agency. Most migrations take 4–12 weeks from planning to launch. Our [ecommerce platform migration guide](/ecommerce-platform-migration-guide) walks through the full process.

SEO risk is the biggest concern. URL structure differences between Shopify and BigCommerce mean you need complete 301 redirect mapping. Miss those redirects and you can lose months of organic traffic recovery. Export your full URL list, map every old URL to its new equivalent, and watch Google Search Console for crawl errors daily for the first two weeks after launch.

Both platforms offer built-in CSV import tools. But for stores with 1,000+ products, third-party services like Cart2Cart or LitExtension handle the heavy lifting more reliably. Budget $300–$1,000 for automated migration services on top of any agency fees.

Don't migrate over small frustrations. Platform switches should align with a 3–5 year business plan—not a reaction to one missing feature that an app could solve. Merchants who migrate reactively often underestimate how much the transition disrupts daily operations and marketing workflows.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Shopify charge transaction fees in 2025?
Yes. Shopify charges 2% (Basic), 1% (Shopify), or 0.5% (Advanced) per transaction unless you use Shopify Payments. BigCommerce charges zero transaction fees on all plans regardless of payment gateway. (Source: Shopify.com, 2025)

### Is BigCommerce better for SEO than Shopify?
They're close. Both offer solid SEO foundations with clean URLs, sitemaps, and metadata control. BigCommerce offers slightly more URL flexibility, while Shopify's speed advantage via its CDN and OS 2.0 themes can offset any structural differences. Neither has a decisive SEO edge for most merchants.

### Can BigCommerce handle large product catalogs better than Shopify?
In most cases, yes. BigCommerce supports up to 600 SKU variants per product natively versus Shopify's 100-variant limit (extendable via apps). For merchants with complex product options or large catalogs, BigCommerce is often the stronger native fit. (Source: BigCommerce.com, 2025)

### Which platform is easier to use for beginners?
Shopify. Its dashboard, theme editor, and onboarding flow are consistently rated more beginner-friendly, earning a 4.4/5 ease-of-use score on G2 versus BigCommerce's 4.2/5. BigCommerce has a steeper learning curve and often requires developer help for customization. (Source: G2.com, 2026)

### Does BigCommerce force you to upgrade plans as your revenue grows?
Yes. BigCommerce Standard is capped at $50K/year GMV, Plus at $180K, and Pro at $400K. Exceeding those thresholds means upgrading to the next plan. Shopify has no GMV-based plan limits. (Source: BigCommerce.com, 2025)

### Which platform is better for B2B ecommerce in 2025?
Both have improved. BigCommerce B2B Edition offers native customer groups, quote management, and net payment terms on lower-cost plans. Shopify Plus B2B (launched 2022, expanded 2024) now covers most core B2B use cases but requires $2,300+/month pricing. BigCommerce still has the edge for complex wholesale scenarios without heavy app reliance, though Shopify Plus is closing the gap.

### Is Shopify Plus worth the cost over BigCommerce Enterprise?
It depends on your priorities. Shopify Plus at ~$2,300/month delivers a smoother merchant experience, stronger social commerce, and the largest app ecosystem. BigCommerce Enterprise suits merchants who need zero transaction fees, larger API quotas, and multi-storefront without added cost. The only reliable way to decide: request demos and quotes from both, then run a total-cost-of-ownership calculation for your specific tech stack.
